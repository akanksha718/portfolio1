The Art of Giving an Impressive Introduction

On first day we learnt that Your introduction is the gateway to how others perceive you, whether in a professional meeting, a casual gathering, or even online. Mastering this skill can leave a lasting impression and set the tone for any interaction. and also various tips for a Impressive introduction

1. Start with a Smile: A warm smile conveys confidence and friendliness. It puts you and your audience at ease.

2. Make Eye Contact: Eye contact shows that you are engaged and interested in the conversation. Avoid staring but maintain a natural gaze.

3. Keep It Concise: Your introduction should be brief, ideally under 30 seconds. Avoid unnecessary details.

week 3

Movie review: Forest Grump

I recently watched Forrest Gump, directed by Robert Zemeckis, and it honestly left a strong impression on me. As a student, especially someone still figuring out life, I found Forrest’s story incredibly inspiring and emotional. The movie follows the life of Forrest Gump, a man with a low IQ but a heart full of kindness and determination.
What stood out to me most was how Forrest never lets his limitations define him. Despite being different, he achieves amazing things — from becoming a college football star, serving in the Vietnam War, starting a shrimp business, and even running across the country. His innocence and honesty make him a character you can’t help but love. It taught me that you don’t have to be the smartest person in the room to make a difference — you just have to keep moving forward, like he says, "Life is like a box of chocolates. You never know what you're gonna get."
The way the movie ties Forrest’s life to real historical events was also really cool. As a student learning about those events in history class, seeing Forrest interact with moments like meeting presidents or being part of major movements made it more interesting and relatable.
Overall, Forrest Gump is not just a great film — it’s a life lesson wrapped in an emotional, funny, and unforgettable story. I would definitely recommend it to other students because it teaches empathy, perseverance, and the importance of staying true to yourself
